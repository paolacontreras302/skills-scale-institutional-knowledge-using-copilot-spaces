# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure the product is usable, accessible, and visually consistent. They collaborate with Product Managers to refine requirements, work with Developers to implement designs, and run user research or usability tests to validate solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, and prototypes
- Lead user research activities and translate findings into product improvements
- Develop and maintain design system standards
- Collaborate with Developers on implementation fidelity and review

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible experiences that meet user needs
- Reduce friction in the user journey
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Regular design reviews with Product Managers and Engineering
- User testing summaries shared with Product and Project Managers
- Handoff documentation (e.g., annotated mockups) delivered to Developers before sprint start

---

## Quality Analyst (QA)

### Role Summary
Quality Analysts uphold the quality standards of the delivery team. They work with Developers to define acceptance criteria, participate in risk reviews, and maintain a focus on user value throughout the delivery lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and document acceptance criteria for critical features
- Build test plans and execute manual or automated tests
- Track and report defects and quality metrics
- Participate in release go/no-go decisions and release notes

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Ensure every shippable increment meets the Definition of Done
- Continuously improve test coverage and automation

### Typical Communication
- Test reports shared in sprint demos and release meetings
- Incident postmortems with Developers and Project Managers
- Coordination with DevOps Engineers on test environment readiness

---

## Customer Success Lead

### Role Summary
Customer Success Leads ensure customer needs are met after launch. They conduct onboarding, collect user feedback, and surface pain points to the product team so that the backlog reflects real-world usage.

### Responsibilities
- Lead onboarding sessions for new users
- Gather feedback and usage insights from customers
- Advocate for customer-focused improvements with Product and Project Managers
- Coordinate training materials and help documentation

### Goals
- Drive product adoption and customer satisfaction
- Reduce time-to-value for new users
- Create a feedback loop between customers and the delivery team

### Typical Communication
- Regular feedback sessions with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Customer escalation handoffs coordinated with Project Managers
- Input to retrospectives on customer-reported issues

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and operational health. They work with Developers to create maintainable CI/CD pipelines, ensure secure and scalable releases, and respond to operational incidents.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and optimize CI/CD processes and deployment automation
- Collaborate with Developers on release strategies and environment configuration
- Monitor system health and lead incident response
- Enforce security and compliance standards in the delivery pipeline

### Goals
- Enable frequent, low-risk deployments
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) during incidents
- Maintain environment parity and infrastructure reliability

### Typical Communication
- Release planning sessions with Developers and Project Managers
- Incident review and postmortem meetings with QA and Developers
- Capacity and infrastructure planning with Project Managers

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts enable data-driven decisions by providing insights, dashboards, and ad hoc analyses. They support product planning and retrospectives by surfacing trends in usage, performance, and business outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain product dashboards and reporting pipelines
- Run ad hoc analyses on product performance and user behavior
- Support retrospectives and planning sessions with data insights
- Identify opportunities for process optimization through metrics

### Goals
- Make data accessible and actionable for all roles
- Improve decision quality through evidence-based recommendations
- Proactively surface anomalies or trends that affect delivery

### Typical Communication
- Present findings to Product Managers and Project Managers during planning and retrospective meetings
- Collaborate with Developers on instrumentation and data collection
- Share dashboards and reports with Customer Success Lead to inform onboarding materials
